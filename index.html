the fun palace

Inspired by low fidelity sketches of Cedric Price and Joan Littlewood (1964), The Fun Palace (A Carnival of Mixed Realities) was a one-night only event that proposed new audience interactions with emerging technologies such as Augmented Reality (AR), Virtual Reality (VR), and Mixed Reality (MR). We were tasked to design and implement the AR "layer" of the event - interactions that would bring together the varying installations cohesively.

Project Team: Gabriel Reis, Alex Castelan, Li Yi, Lujia Yang, Riya Chak, Zehra Khan, Grace Chang

femmes interactive

F.E.M.M.E.S., or Femmes Engaging Multimedia and Envolving Systems, is a series of workshops that aims to support womxn, nonbinary, and femmes of colour in multi-media/interactive storytelling. Last fall, I had the pleasure of developing and leading a beginner programming workshop with FEMMES Interactive. I taught programming basics using Python, and developed a simple arcade game using the Python Arcade Library for the participants.

the little prince

An unknowing astronaut has found the Little Princeâ€™s Rose and took it back to Earth as proof of life in space. Now, the Little Prince has to travel across the galaxy back to Earth in order to get his Rose back! Playing as the Little Prince, you must navigate each planet he visits and find a key to leave the planet and travel onto the next one. As you advance through each planet, the number of enemies increase. Fight them by collecting stars!

Created for CPSC 427, I worked on the game's collision handling, state machine, asset design/creation, animating the game intro, and project management.

directed studies: scholars vr

Led by Dr. Patrick Pennefather, Scholars VR aims to make scholarly writing more accessible and interesting using immersive technologies such as VR. I am currently doing a directed studies with Dr. Pennefather, focusing on VR interaction development and UI/UX research.
